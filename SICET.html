Motivation Behind The Workshop

As deep learning models become increasingly complex, deploying them on resource-constrained devices poses significant challenges. Tiny Machine Learning (TinyML) enables ultra-compact, low-power AI models suitable for embedded systems and real-time sensor analytics.

This workshop covers pruning, quantization-aware training, post-training quantization, knowledge distillation, and efficient network design strategies, with practical deployment on embedded/mobile platforms.

1. Academic Research Gap in Sri Lanka

We compared metadata from five Sri Lankan IEEE conferences (ICAC, ICARC, ICIIS, MERCon, SCSE) and four international IEEE venues (AICAS, EDGE, ISCAS, PERCOM) to show the gap. Code/data: GitHub.

2. Democratization of AI

TinyML lowers the cost and hardware needs for inference, enabling AI at the edge with privacy and low latency.

3. Relevance to Core Engineering Domains

Civil: On-device SHM; Biomedical: Wearable diagnostics locally; Electrical/Computer: Smart meters, grid monitoring; Mechanical: Predictive maintenance at the edge.

4. Consumer Product Engineering

Always-on sensing (e.g., wake-word detection) and offline operation benefit from TinyML’s power efficiency and privacy.
